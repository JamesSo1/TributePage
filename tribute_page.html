Stephen Hillenburg

The Man Who Shaped The Childhoods of Millions

Brief Time Line of Stephen Hillenburg's Life:

  • 1961 - Born in Lawton, Oklahoma.
  • 1984 - Starts teaching marine biology to the public at the Orange County Marine Institute after receiving a degree in natural-resource planning and interpretation from Humboldt State University.
  • 1987 - Stops teaching marine biology at the Orange County Marine Institute.
  • 1989 - Enrolls in the California Institute of the Arts to pursue animation.
  • 1991 - Creates the short film, The Green Beret.
  • 1992 - Creates the short film, Wormholes.
  • 1995-96 - Works as the creative director of the hit Nickelodeon show, Rocko's Modern Life.
  • 1999 - The first episode of SpongeBob SquarePants premieres on Nickelodeon
  • 2004 - The Spongebob SquarePants Movie is released and Hillenburg resigns as the showrunner of SpongeBob SquarePants.
  • 2017 - Reveals that he has been diagnosed with ALS.
  • 2018 - Passes away from ALS at the age of 57.
